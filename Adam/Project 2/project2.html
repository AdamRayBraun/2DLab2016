As London continued to expand South in the 1800s, almost all of the tributaries responsible for keeping South London from being a flood plain were built over. Many of these Rivers were once landmarks of the areas they ran through but now their names are rarely mentioned.

The River Effra is one of those small underground tributaries to the River Thames. It runs from the hills of south London North to meet the Thames in Vauxhall. It is one of many subterranean rivers of itsâ€™ kind now only known by the people that live above it.
This is a visualization of all of the buildings, objects or infrastructure still named after the River Effra.The lasting influence of these rivers can be found all over London from Peckham being names after the River Peck to Ravensbourne being named after the River Ravensbourne. This piece aims to help look back in time into what was once a major part of the landscape of London.
